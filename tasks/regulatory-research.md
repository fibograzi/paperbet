## 1. FTC (US) Affiliate Disclosure Requirements

### Legal Basis

- **FTC Act, Section 5** — Prohibits "unfair or deceptive acts or practices in or affecting commerce"
- **FTC Endorsement Guides** (16 CFR Part 255) — Updated 2023, actively enforced 2025-2026
- **CAN-SPAM Act** — Governs commercial email marketing

### Core Requirements

All **material connections** between an endorser and an advertiser must be disclosed. A "material connection" includes:
- Payments or commissions (including affiliate revenue)
- Free products or services
- Business/family relationships
- The possibility of being paid or winning a prize

### Disclosure Standards ("Clear and Conspicuous")

Disclosures must be:
- **Difficult to miss** (easily noticeable) and **easily understandable** by ordinary consumers
- **Unavoidable** in interactive electronic media (websites, social media)
- **Not hidden** behind "more" links, in fine print, or requiring scrolling
- **Placed as close as possible** to the endorsement/affiliate link
- **Present every time** an affiliate link or endorsement appears (not just on a single page)

### What Must Be Disclosed for PaperBet

- That PaperBet earns a commission when users click affiliate links and sign up at casinos
- The nature of the relationship with each casino partner (Stake, Rollbit, BC.Game, etc.)
- That users are clicking through to **real-money gambling platforms**
- For email marketing: clear unsubscribe mechanism (CAN-SPAM requires honoring opt-outs within 10 business days)

### Recommended Disclosure Language Examples

- "This site contains affiliate links. If you sign up through our links, we may earn a commission at no extra cost to you."
- "Affiliate Disclosure: PaperBet.io earns referral commissions from the crypto casinos listed on this site."
- Must appear **above the fold**, near affiliate links, not just in footer or separate page

### Penalties

- **$53,088 per violation** (2025 amount, adjusted annually for inflation)
- **Each individual ad/post/page** with missing/inadequate disclosure = separate violation
- Enforcement actions include: warning letters, cease-and-desist orders, civil monetary fines, injunctive relief
- FTC actively monitors and investigates deceptive advertising

### Gambling-Specific FTC Requirements

- **Age verification disclosures**: Must state gambling is restricted to adults (18+ or 21+ depending on jurisdiction)
- **Responsible gambling messaging**: Statements like "Gambling involves risk. Please gamble responsibly" plus links to resources (e.g., National Council on Problem Gambling, 1-800-GAMBLER)
- **No misleading claims**: Cannot imply typical results or guaranteed winnings; losses must be acknowledged as common
- **Truth-in-advertising**: Cannot misrepresent odds of winning or promotion terms

## 2. UK ASA/UKGC Gambling Advertising Regulations

### Regulatory Bodies

- **UK Gambling Commission (UKGC)** — Licenses and regulates gambling operators and enforces compliance
- **Advertising Standards Authority (ASA)** — Enforces the CAP Code for advertising standards
- **Committee of Advertising Practice (CAP)** — Writes the advertising codes

### Affiliate Licensing

- Affiliates **do not need a separate UKGC license** in the UK
- However, **licensed operators are fully responsible** for all affiliate activity — affiliates are treated as extensions of the operator's marketing team
- Operators must ensure affiliate compliance with all UKGC and ASA rules
- Non-compliant affiliate activity can result in sanctions against the **operator's license**

### Key CAP Code Rules (Updated September/October 2025)

**Scope expansion (September 2025):**
- CAP Code rules now apply to **all gambling operators targeting UK consumers**, regardless of where the operator is based
- Social media marketing from licensed operators targeting UK consumers is in scope
- **Any third-party material** (including affiliate content) promoting a licensed gambling product is covered

**Content restrictions:**
- Ads must be **obviously identifiable as advertising** — use labels like "Ad" if not clear from context
- **Prohibition on celebrity, athlete, and influencer endorsements** in gambling ads
- **Age appeal test**: Content must not appeal to under-25s (raised from under-18s)
- **No "strong appeal" to young persons** — restrictions on animation, gaming culture references, sport personalities, social media aesthetics
- **No misleading bonus claims** or incentive advertising without clear terms

**October 2025 guidance update:**
- Replaced 2022 guidance
- Clarifies "strong appeal" test across sport, music, animation, social media, and game design
- Stricter interpretation of what constitutes appeal to younger audiences

### UKGC Marketing Rules (2025-2026)

**From May 2025:**
- Operators may only directly market to customers who have **consented on a granular basis** — per product (casino, bingo, betting) AND per channel (SMS, email)

**From January 2026:**
- New rules on **incentive offers** take effect
- Maximum **10x wagering requirements** on bonuses
- **Ban on mixed-product promotional offers** (e.g., "bet on sports, get free casino spins")
- Mandatory **wagering requirement calculators** for promotional offers

### Stake.com UK Exit

- Stake.com exited the UK market in early 2025 due to UKGC regulatory pressure and investigations into social media advertising
- **Risk for PaperBet**: Promoting Stake to UK users could be promoting an unlicensed operator in the UK

## 3. EU Gambling Advertising Laws

### EU-Wide Framework

- **No EU-wide gambling advertising legislation** — each member state regulates independently
- EU Treaty on the Functioning of the EU (TFEU) provides baseline principles
- **MiCA framework** (Markets in Crypto-Assets) adds requirements for crypto-based gambling operations
- General trend: **tightening advertising restrictions** across Europe in 2025-2026

### Netherlands (KSA)

**Current rules:**
- **Ban on untargeted gambling advertising** (since July 2023)
- Affiliate marketers must prove **95% of their audience is 24 years or older**
- **Sports sponsorship fully banned** from July 2025
- **Cashback bonuses prohibited**
- No separate affiliate license required, but **KSA actively investigates affiliate websites**

**2025 enforcement actions:**
- KSA **permanently took down Casinoscout.nl** for promoting illegal casinos
- Owners face fines of **€75,000 per week** (max €225,000 per company) for continued violations
- Domain was seized via the Dutch domain registry (SIDN)
- KSA specifically targeted a "Best Unlicensed Casinos 2025" page with operator logos and "Play with us" buttons

**Risk for PaperBet**: Very high. Promoting crypto casinos (most are not KSA-licensed) to Dutch users could trigger enforcement. The "Deal Wheel" feature directing users to casinos resembles the type of promotional activity KSA is actively cracking down on.

### Belgium

**Current rules:**
- **Broad ban on all gambling advertising** since 2023 — covers public spaces, TV, radio, cinema, social media, and online
- **Prohibition on free games, gaming credits, and any incentive** offered to influence gambling behavior or attract/retain players (Law of 18 February 2024, effective September 2024)
- **Minimum age raised to 21** for all games of chance (including table games, betting, automatic games)
- Ban includes bonuses, free games, cashback, welcome packages, travel, meals, drinks, or any gifts below market price

**Risk for PaperBet**: Extremely high. Free casino simulators that funnel users to real casinos would likely violate Belgium's ban on incentives designed to influence gambling behavior. The entire PaperBet model — free games as a gateway to real gambling — could be considered an illegal incentive under Belgian law.

### Germany (GlüStV 2021)

**Current rules:**
- Interstate Treaty on Gambling (Glücksspielstaatsvertrag 2021) governs
- **Ban on revenue-share affiliate compensation** — no variable remuneration (revenue, deposit, or stakes-based) may be paid for internet advertising, including affiliate links
- **Advertising watershed**: No advertising for virtual slot games, online poker, or casino between 6:00 AM and 9:00 PM via TV, radio, or internet
- **Ban on influencer advertising** beyond current scope
- **Restrictions on bonus offers**

**Mandatory disclosures for affiliates:**
- Prohibition of minor participation (18+)
- Risk of addiction warnings (e.g., "Participation from 18; gambling can be addictive")
- Availability of operator-independent counseling and therapy
- Disclosure that the affiliate receives remuneration if a player registers

**Risk for PaperBet**: High. The revenue-share ban on affiliate compensation, combined with advertising time restrictions, creates major compliance issues. The "What You Would Have Won" display could be seen as advertising casino games during restricted hours.

### Italy (Decreto Dignità)

**Current rules:**
- **Complete ban on all gambling advertising** across traditional and digital media (Decreto Dignità, 2018)
- Affiliates cannot use: direct call-to-action language ("Play Now," "Deposit Today," "Claim Your Bonus"), bonus/promotional highlighting, testimonials, success stories, or sponsorship
- Affiliates **may** create responsible gambling guides, explain odds, compare operators on neutral criteria (licensing, payment methods), and provide industry analysis
- **Penalties**: €5,000 to €500,000 depending on severity and frequency (AGCOM enforcement)

**Possible future change**: Discussions about controlled advertising model for 2026-2027, but ban remains active as of March 2026.

**Risk for PaperBet**: Extremely high. The entire site model (affiliate links, "Deal Wheel," promotional casino listings) violates Italy's advertising ban.

## 4. Gambling Affiliate Licensing Requirements

### Countries/Jurisdictions Requiring Affiliate Licenses

| Jurisdiction | License Required? | Regulatory Body | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Romania** | **Yes** — Class 2 License | ONJN (National Gambling Office) | Must be EU/EEA/Switzerland entity; formal agreements with licensed operators required |
| **Greece** | **Yes** — Special license | HGC (Hellenic Gaming Commission) | Required to promote licensed local operators |
| **US (NJ, PA, MI)** | **Yes** — Registration/License | State gaming commissions | Type depends on business model (CPA, RevShare); ranges from simplified registration to full license |
| **UK** | **No** (but operator responsible) | UKGC | Affiliates operate under operator's license; operator bears full compliance responsibility |
| **Netherlands** | **No** (but strict rules) | KSA | Must prove 95% of audience is 24+; active enforcement against affiliate sites |
| **Germany** | **No** (but strict rules) | State authorities | Revenue-share compensation banned; mandatory disclosures |
| **Spain** | **No** (but can be sanctioned) | DGOJ | Affiliates can be sanctioned under Royal Decree 958/2020 for facilitating gambling ads |
| **Belgium** | **N/A** | Gaming Commission | All gambling advertising banned — affiliate marketing effectively prohibited |
| **Italy** | **N/A** | AGCOM/ADM | All gambling advertising banned — affiliate marketing effectively prohibited |

### Key Takeaway

Even in jurisdictions where a separate affiliate license is not required, affiliates are subject to the same advertising and responsible gambling standards as the operators they promote. Regulators hold operators responsible for affiliate conduct, creating indirect compliance obligations.

## 5. Google AdSense / Ad Network Policies

### Google AdSense

**Gambling content restrictions:**
- AdSense **will not approve** gambling sites for monetization
- Sites promoting real-money gambling or linking to gambling operators face **rejection or account suspension**

**Social casino game policy:**
- Affiliates and aggregators are **explicitly prohibited** from promoting social casino games (as of late 2025)
- Violations result in **immediate account suspension without prior warning**

**Certification requirements (March 2026 update):**
- All accounts advertising gambling must demonstrate "good policy health"
- Must own and operate a second-level domain (no subdomains on third-party platforms)

**Sweepstakes reclassification (October 2025):**
- Sweepstakes casinos now classified as **real-money gambling**
- Subject to same restrictions: operator licensing, country-specific certification, responsible gambling messaging
- If any virtual currency exchanges for gift cards, crypto, or cash, it's classified as gambling

### Google Ads (for running ads TO the site)

- Gambling ad certification required per country
- Only available in markets where gambling is legally allowed
- Affiliates must comply with same responsible gambling standards as operators

### Other Ad Networks

| Network | Gambling Policy |
|---|---|
| **Mediavine** | Requires 50,000 monthly sessions; strict content policies; gambling sites likely rejected |
| **Ezoic** | More lenient approval but must be in good standing with AdSense; "questionable niches" flagged |
| **Media.net** | Alternative to AdSense; gambling content policies vary |
| **PropellerAds/Adsterra** | More permissive toward gambling content; lower RPMs |
| **Blockchain-Ads** | Specifically serves crypto/gambling niche |

### Practical Impact for PaperBet

- **Cannot use Google AdSense** for site monetization
- **Cannot run Google Ads** to promote the site without gambling certification (requires operator license)
- Must use gambling-specific or crypto-specific ad networks
- Most premium ad networks will reject the site

## 6. Social Casino / Simulated Gambling Regulations

### US Federal Framework

- **Unlawful Internet Gambling Enforcement Act (UIGEA, 2006)** — Prohibits businesses from accepting payments for illegal internet gambling
- Created the legal gray area around dual-currency systems
- **FTC Act** — Covers deceptive practices in simulated gambling (FTC enforcement action against HoYoverse/Genshin Impact in early 2025 for misleading loot box mechanics)

### 2025 US State Crackdown on Sweepstakes/Social Casinos

**The US social casino market generated ~$3.4 billion in 2024, triggering a major enforcement wave in 2025:**

| State | Action | Key Details |
|---|---|---|
| **Connecticut** | **Banned** | SB 1235 — criminalizes operation or promotion of games that simulate gambling; violations = unfair/deceptive business practices |
| **Montana** | **Banned** | Signed May 2025 — bans any online casino allowing bets using "any form of currency" with payouts |
| **New Jersey** | **Banned** | AB 5447 signed August 2025 — bans sweepstakes-style casino platforms |
| **New York** | **Banned + enforcement** | AG issued cease-and-desist to 26 platforms; SB 5935A prohibits online sweepstakes simulating casino games using dual-currency systems |
| **California** | **Banned** | AB 831 signed October 2025 — prohibits online sweepstakes games using dual-currency model; **criminal misdemeanor** with up to 1 year in jail and $25,000 per violation |
| **Louisiana** | **Banned** | Passed legislation prohibiting sweepstakes casino games |
| **Delaware, Maryland, Michigan** | **Enforcement** | Sent cease-and-desist orders to sweepstakes operators |

### Washington State

- **RCW 9.46** — All gambling is illegal unless specifically authorized
- Virtual currency has been recognized as a **"thing of value"** in WA gambling law
- Games of chance wagering virtual currency for more virtual currency **likely constitute illegal gambling**
- Social casino apps using virtual currency with **no cash value** may be legal — but if the app allows purchasing additional chips or converting to real money, it **likely violates state law**
- Washington is one of the most restrictive states for social/simulated gambling

### Belgium

- **All free games, gaming credits, and incentives** designed to influence gambling behavior or attract/retain players are **prohibited** (Law of 18 February 2024, effective September 2024)
- This effectively bans the social casino model as a gateway to real gambling
- Minimum gambling age raised to **21**

### PaperBet-Specific Analysis: Are FREE Casino Simulators Regulated?

**Critical distinction**: PaperBet's games use only virtual money with no cash-out — BUT they are explicitly designed to funnel users to real-money casinos. This creates regulatory risk:

1. **If classified as purely free games with no value exchange**: Generally legal in most jurisdictions as entertainment
2. **If classified as promotional/inducement tools for real gambling**: Subject to gambling advertising regulations in all jurisdictions
3. **"What You Would Have Won" displays**: These show real money amounts, creating a direct bridge between the free game and real gambling. This is likely to be viewed by regulators as:
   - An **inducement to gamble** (violates Belgium, Italy, and potentially other jurisdictions)
   - **Potentially deceptive advertising** under FTC standards (implies achievable winnings without balanced risk disclosure)
   - A form of **untargeted gambling advertising** (violates Netherlands KSA rules)

### Minimum Compliance Actions Required

To operate legally in ANY jurisdiction, PaperBet would need at minimum:

1. **Clear affiliate disclosures** on every page with affiliate links (FTC compliance)
2. **Age verification/gate** (18+ or 21+ depending on jurisdiction)
3. **Responsible gambling messaging** on every page ("Gambling involves risk," helpline numbers)
4. **Geo-blocking** for jurisdictions where the site model is illegal (Belgium, Italy, multiple US states)
5. **GDPR-compliant consent** for email collection (separate consents for data processing and marketing)
6. **"What You Would Have Won" removal or modification** — this feature creates deceptive advertising risk
7. **Deal Wheel modification** — must not function as a gambling mechanic; needs clear disclaimers
8. **Operator licensing verification** — must verify each casino partner is licensed in each target jurisdiction
9. **No promotion of unlicensed operators** in regulated markets (e.g., Stake in UK)
10. **Cookie consent and privacy policy** compliant with GDPR/PECR
